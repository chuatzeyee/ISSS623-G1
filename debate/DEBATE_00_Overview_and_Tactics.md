# Mini Structured Debate on Ageing - Overview, Format & Tactics

**Motion: "Population ageing should primarily be framed as a healthcare and economic challenge."**

**Scenario:** Singapore is becoming a super-aged society. Your team advises the Government (PMO, MTI, MOH) on how to frame ageing in a **national strategy document**. The learning outcome: explain how perceptions of demographic change shape **policy priorities, public attitudes, and healthcare planning**.

Central question: *Is population ageing best understood as a dependency problem, a healthcare planning problem, or a longevity opportunity?*

## Format (25–30 min total)

| Time | Activity | What YOU do |
|---|---|---|
| 3 min | Read article extract; individual reflection | Highlight 2–3 stats you will actually quote; jot your 2 strongest points |
| 5 min | Groups A & B discuss; Group C prepares evaluation criteria | Assign speaking order fast (see below); converge on 3 arguments max |
| 4 min | Group A presents | ~3 speakers × 75 sec, or opener/body/closer |
| 4 min | Group B presents | Same; ideally rebut A's specific points, not a canned speech |
| 5 min | Group C discusses & synthesizes | A & B: stay quiet, note what C picks up on |
| 4 min | Group C presents synthesis + instructor wrap-up | - |

Groups sit together. Roles: **A = Proposition** (ageing is mainly a healthcare & fiscal challenge), **B = Opposition** (ageing should be framed as a social & community asset), **C = Policy Jury** (evaluate both, propose balanced framing - NOT just pick a winner).

## The Stat Card (memorize - usable by every group)

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| SG residents aged 65+ | 20.7% (2025) → **23.9% by 2030** (~1 in 4) |
| "Super-aged" threshold (UN/WHO convention) | 21% aged 65+ - SG crosses ~2026 |
| Life expectancy at birth | **83.9 years** (2025); healthy life expectancy ≈ 74 → ~10 years in poor health |
| Old-age support ratio (residents 20–64 per 65+) | **5.7 (2015) → 2.7 (2030)** |
| National health expenditure | **$22B (2018) → $59B (2030)** projected |
| Healthcare workforce | 129k (2024) → **~156k needed by 2030** (+20%) |
| Global context | WHO projects **18M health-worker shortfall by 2030** |

## Key vocabulary (use precisely - it signals preparation)

- **Aged / super-aged society**: 14% / 21% of population aged 65+.
- **Old-age dependency ratio** vs **old-age support ratio**: the same fraction inverted; "dependency" language itself embeds the deficit framing (a point Group B can exploit).
- **Silver tsunami**: alarmist metaphor for the ageing wave.
- **Compression of morbidity**: pushing illness into a shorter window at the end of life; the gap between lifespan and healthspan.
- **Deficit-based vs asset-based framing**: seniors as consumers of resources vs contributors of resources.
- **Intergenerational capital**: knowledge, care, and wealth transfers from older to younger generations.
- **Longevity economy / longevity dividend**: economic value created by and for older adults.

## Delivery tactics (this is 15%-participation territory)

1. **Structure beats volume.** Open with a one-line thesis, number your arguments ("three reasons"), close by restating. 4 minutes ≈ 550 words total for a group - brutal; cap at 3 arguments.
2. **Quote Singapore numbers, not generalities.** "One in four Singaporeans will be 65+ by 2030" lands harder than "the population is ageing."
3. **Pre-empt the other side** in one sentence per argument ("Some will say X - but…"). The jury's criteria almost always include anticipation of counterarguments.
4. **Speak to the scenario**: you're advising the Government on a *strategy document*. Argue about consequences of the FRAMING (what gets funded, what the public believes, what gets measured) - not just whether ageing is good or bad. This is the actual point of the exercise.
5. **Tie to the course** if you get a closing line: framing determines which data we collect and which analytics we build (challenge framing → cost prediction and bed-demand models; asset framing → healthy-ageing, participation, and prevention analytics). The instructor will love this.
6. In the 5-min huddle: 30 sec to pick a leader, 2 min to choose 3 arguments, 90 sec to assign speakers, 1 min dry-run of the opening line.
